# Partner Booth Playbook

## Primary Submission Pitch

- Lead with Quorum Lab as a research-first coordination layer for named, human-aware, evidence-backed forecast receipts.
- Primary submission stack: ENS for readable identity and naming, World for human verification, and Sui/Walrus for durable demo artifacts and receipt storage.
- Keep the claim concrete: the ETHGlobal submission demonstrates how a forecast or governance signal can be named, verified, and archived for review.
- Avoid positioning Blink/Unlink/Dynamic as the main build. They are supporting partner-lab artifacts in the Antigravity monorepo side lane.

## ENS Booth Ask

- Ask for one real agent namespace path: a sponsored parent name, delegated subname, or registration credit for `agents.<parent>.eth`.
- Ask what exact ENSIP-25/ENSIP-26 evidence they want in the Sunday booth demo.
- Show the current live resolver first with `vitalik.eth`, then explain that the remaining unlock is activating one DreamNet agent subname with real text records.
- Ask for help making `liquidity-scout-00001.agents.<parent>.eth` real during the event.

## World Booth Ask

- Ask how World ID should be framed for forecast participation, quorum voting, or receipt review without overclaiming sybil resistance.
- Ask which proof flow is most appropriate for a hackathon demo: sign-in, gating, verification before contribution, or verification before receipt finalization.
- Ask whether the submission should emphasize verified-human participation or abuse-resistant quorum formation.

## Sui/Walrus Booth Demo

- Run a Quorum receipt, click **Store receipt on Walrus**, and show the returned Testnet blob ID.
- Open the aggregator read URL and show the raw receipt JSON loading from Walrus.
- Point to the matching local and read-back SHA-256 hashes.
- Explain Sui/Walrus as the storage and availability lane for agent evidence that should be inspectable after the live demo.
- Keep the demo fallback ready: the verified blob `KPgDNJskxxa9uysu3pCe1lOkfd6MQlx8x6HsIljMOJw` if booth network conditions are unstable.

## Blink/Unlink Side Conversations

- Treat Blink/Unlink/Dynamic as partner-lab side conversations, not the primary ETHGlobal submission.
- Use them to discuss how agent modules could support partner-specific onboarding, account linking, unlinking, and identity recovery patterns.
- Be explicit that these modules are supporting partner-lab artifacts being built by Antigravity in the monorepo side lane.

## Do Not Claim Unless Verified

- Do not claim production readiness, mainnet settlement, live trading, broker access, exchange access, wallet signing, paid API integration, or public social posting unless it has been verified in the current submission workspace.
- Do not claim ENS, World, Sui, Walrus, Blink, Unlink, or Dynamic sponsor approval unless a sponsor representative has said so directly.
- Do not claim Blink/Unlink/Dynamic are part of the primary submission; they are supporting partner-lab artifacts only.
- Do not present stored artifacts, receipts, or proofs as immutable, audited, or final unless the exact implementation has been tested and can be shown during judging.
